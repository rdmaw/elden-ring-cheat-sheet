Tutorial (Level 1-10)

  • (Optional) Pick any starting class you want. If you're not sure, I recommend starting with Vagabond for beginners.
  • (Optional) For a completionist's run, I recommend starting with the Stonesword Key x2 keepsake. They are quite expensive, so you won't have to buy that many of them early on.
  • Start your playthrough, and pick up Tarnished's Wizened Finger from the chapel.
  • The Ring gesture is aqcuired if you pre-ordered the game.
  • Similarly, the Ring of Miquella gesture is aqcuired if you pre-ordered the DLC.
  • Continue toward the boss, and either beat or die to the Grafted Scion.
    • If you beat the boss, continue ahead and inspect the nascent butterfly at the edge of the cliff.
    • If you didn't beat the boss, don't worry, you're supposed to come back here later.
  • After respawning in the cave, take the plunge down to Cave of Knowledge.
  • Rest at the grace, and allocate your flask charges.
  • There are no items in this cave, so once you are done, kill Soldier of Godrick for 400 runes.
  • Continue ahead and pick up the Strength! gesture before you drop down.
  • Drop down and activate the Stranded Graveyard grace.
  • Pick up Finger Severer and Tarnished's Furled Finger in one pickup, near the exit lift.
  • Take the lift up, and enter the Lands Between!

Early Liurnia and Caelid (Level 1-25)

  • Reminder that you should try to avoid fighting any enemies while you are in Liurnia and Caelid early. You will pretty much get one-shot by most enemies, so just run through each step without fighting anything.
  • Travel to Stormhill Shack, and follow the path up until you get to a scarab in the middle of the road. Be wary of the ballistas and kill the scarab to get Ash of War: Wild Strikes.
  • From the scarab's initial position, head northwest and on the right side under the bridge ahead is a corpse with Lump of Flesh.
  • Continue north toward the bridge. You can talk to the Finger Reader here if you want, but continue past the bridge for Nomadic Warrior's Cookbook [7].
  • Jump down from the bridge and southwest before you continue any further is a scarab you can kill for Ash of War: Storm Wall.
  • Turn around and head east up the rocks and follow the path until you reach the Lake-Facing Cliffs site of grace.
  • If you rest at the grace here, or any other in Liurnia, Irina will die and Hyetta will spawn here in front of the grace. You can talk to her, but we don't have any shabiri grapes yet.
  • Take in the view and once you're ready, head southwest to the church below. Head inside the church and pick up Sacred Tear.
  • Talk to Thops, give him 10 runes to unlock his inventory, and buy any sorceries you need from him:
    • Glintstone Pebble: 1,000 runes
    • Glintstone Arc: 1,500 runes
    • Starlight: 1,500 runes
  • Continue west from the church toward a graveyard and pick up Warming Stone at the base of the tree.
  • Jump across the gap toward the other side of the graveyard and pick up Academy Scroll in front of the big gravestone.
    • Now that you've obtained your first scroll, you have the option of giving it to Sellen, Seluvis, or Miriel to unlock the scroll's sorceries for purchase.
    • Similarly, once you get your first prayerbook, you will have the option of giving it to either Corhyn or Miriel to unlock that prayerbook's incantations for purchase.
    • Since Sellen, Corhyn, and Seluvis are often unavailable due to their quests, I recommend you give all the scrolls and prayerbooks you find to Miriel instead, as he is always there for youâ¤ï¸. Doing this will also keep all your purchasable spells in one place.

Vigor: The stat you should focus on early. Getting 40 Vigor early makes the entire early-mid game a complete joke. Combined with the fact you can start the game with a +5 Somber weapon and plenty of upgraded Flasks, you will be tanking and killing most enemies with little investment. It is not recommended to level Vigor past 60.

Mind: Although the scaling dropoff on Mind happens quite late, most builds will be fine around 20-40 Mind.

Endurance: Level based on how much stamina and equip load your build needs. You typically want to end around 40-50 Endurance.

Strength: Strength scaling depends largely on your weapon and it's individual scaling when infused with an ash of war affinity. For an accurate scaling use a build planner, put in your weapon and play with the Strength stat until you notice dropoffs. Don't forget that two-handing your weapon multiplies your Strength stat by 1.5

Dexterity: Same as with Strength. Dex scaling depends on your weapon and it's individual scaling. Use a build planner, put in your weapon of choice and play with the Dexterity stat until you notice dropoffs.

Intelligence: For the individual scaling on each staff, I highly recommend checking out Fextra's page about Intelligence.

Faith: For the individual scaling on each seal, I highly recommend checking out Fextra's page about Faith.

Arcane: If your weapon scales with Arcane, then leveling this stat will increase the amount of Blood Loss, Poison, Sleep, and Madness buildup your weapon inflicts. A list of all weapons that scale with Arcane can be found on Fextra's page. Arcane is also the only stat that increases your Discovery (1 per point).

General recommendation:

  1. Reach level requirements for your desired weapon.
  2. Focus exclusively on vigor. Leveling any of the damage stats yield little value, since most weapons have poor scaling early on.
  3. Level Endurance for your equip load with armor and weapon.
  4. Start focusing on your dedicated stat, once your weapon has better scaling

FAQ

What's included in the playthrough?

The playthrough DOES NOT include:

  • Any farmable items, plants, and crafting materials picked from the ground (materials picked off the wall or ground such as smithing stones or gloveworts ARE included).
  • Any respawning scarabs such as red and blue teardrop scarabs that fill your flasks.
  • Any stranded souls/ghosts or sword monuments that you can interact with for optional lore.
  • Any martyr effigies that you can interact with to activate co-op summon-signs for an area.
  • Any guide and gatekeeper statues, birdseye telescopes, or stakes of marika.
  • Any farmable weapon, shield or armor that drop as loot from enemies are not included in the playthrough, but can be found in the Armaments and Armor tabs respectively.

The playthrough DOES include:

  • Every single item pickup in the game (glowing pickups that you can interact with to receive an item).
  • All bosses in natural order.
  • All NPCs, invasions, and their corresponding questlines in natural order.
  • All merchants and their items in order of when you'd come across them, which you can also track using the merchant filter.
  • All gestures, ashes, paintings and talismans.
  • All scarabs that drop any loot.
  • All illusory walls and secrets, so you won't need to look stupid attacking random walls.

How do I use this website?

This project is intended to be a walkthrough guide of the entire game, containing every single item pickup (excluding plants and materials picked from the ground), NPC questline, boss, and achievement in Elden Ring. If this is your first playthrough, I highly recommend that you play through it normally, then come back and use this guide on your second run.

In the main Playthrough tab, you have a step by step checklist guide through the entire game. You can filter this checklist to only show you steps for the bosses, questlines, achievements or key-items, if you don't want to follow the guide, but still want to get the important stuff.

The Achievements, Armaments, Armor, and Misc tabs can be used as separate checklists to see what weapons/ashes/spells etc., you are missing. Nothing should be missable as long as you're following the main playthrough step-by-step.

In the Resources tab, you can find tables containing useful information about the game's stat caps, upgrade materials, and rune items.

If you don't like the default color mode, you can switch to light or dark mode, by pressing the button with a notebook/sun/moon icon in the navigation bar.

Do I have to follow everything in this exact order?

Nope, this project was primarily intended for me and my cousin. Since I am a completionist, I like to make sure I don't miss a single thing the game has to offer. Elden Ring is a big open world game, so if you want to play at your own pace, but still don't wanna miss any important questlines or achievements, give some of the filters a try.

Can I use this for multiple characters?

Yes! You can create, edit, and delete profiles using the buttons in the navigation bar.

How does the checklist get saved?

The checklists are saved to your browser's local storage. This means that clearing your browser's cache, will destroy any saved progress.

What wiki should I use?

This project is currently using information from Fextralife and Eldenpedia. Fextralife is referenced the most in this cheat sheet, since it is the most complete one. I'd be happy to make the transfer to Eldenpedia in the future, but it seems quite unfinished at this state. For consistency, I recommend you use Fextra's wiki for your own playthrough.

I noticed an error in the cheat sheet! How can I suggest changes?

Visit the project's repository on Github. Check if someone has already reported the same issue, and if not, open a new issue detailing the error. If you want to contribute to the project directly, I recommend you fork the project, make your changes, and open a pull request.
