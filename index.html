FAQ

How do I use the cheat sheet?

This project is a lightweight collection of checklist sheets, covering all items, NPCs, questlines, bosses, achievements and more. If you care about spoilers, I recommend that you experience the game on your own first, then use the cheat sheets for a later playthrough.

Open the menu and select a sheet to get started

Helpful info about soft caps, upgrade tables, rune item values and more can be found in the Resources sheet.

In the Walkthrough sheet, you'll find a step-by-step checklist guide through the entire game, covering as much as possible in a single playthrough. If you're not interested in a guided approach, but still want to make sure you don't miss any important questlines or bosses, try the NPC Walkthrough or Bosses sheets instead.

The Achievements sheet can be used to track achievements and legendary equipment.

Similarly, the Armaments, Armor, Ashes, and Spells sheets can be used to track items in their respective categories. For tracking merchant inventories, use the Merchants sheet.

For miscellaneous items, such as gestures, talismans, cookbooks etc., check out the Key Items and Miscellaneous sheets.

If you're considering starting NG+, make sure to check out the New Game Plus sheet first to see if you've got everything you need.

Configure color themes, import existing profiles and back up your progress in Options.

Active keybinds:

  • Q - Toggle the menu

  • / - Enter search at the top of the page

  • H - Toggle Hide completed steps

What's included in the walkthrough?

The walkthrough DOES NOT COVER:

  • Items that require farming, or respawning plants and crafting materials collected from the environment (e.g., Rowa Fruit, Arteria Leaf).
  • Scarabs that respawn, such as Crimson and Cerulean Tear Scarabs that refill your flasks.
  • Finger Reader Crones, Spirits, or Sword Monuments that players can interact with for lore tidbits.
  • Gatekeeper Statues, Birdseye Telescopes, or Stakes of Marika.
  • Martyr Effigies that players can interact with to activate Co-op summon signs for that area.

The walkthrough COVERS:

  • Every single item pickup in the game.
  • All achievements, NPCs, questlines, bosses and invasions in order of progression.
  • All sites of grace, gestures, ashes of war, spirit ashes, talismans, and paintings.
  • All Ash of War, Glintstone, Incantation, and Somber Scarabs.
  • All Scadutree fragments and Revered Spirit Ashes (DLC).
  • While crafting materials collected from the environment aren't included, all upgrade materials, such as Smithing Stones and Gloveworts are.

Do I have to follow every step in order?

Not at all, this project was primarily intended for my cousin and me. Since I am a completionist, I like to make sure that I don't miss a single detail the game has to offer. The Walkthrough represents how I prefer to complete the game, reducing as much backtracking as possible while keeping a natural order of progression.

Elden Ring is a massive game, so if you'd rather play at your own pace, but don't wanna miss any questlines, try the NPC Walkthrough instead. You can always jump back and forth, checking off tasks in different order, just be mindful that doing some things too early might end certain questlines.

Will I get all achievements if I follow the guide?

Yes, you'll get all achievements by simply following the Walkthrough. No planning needed! If you'd like to track your achievements and legendary items, check out the Achievements sheet. You can also search for specific achievements in the walkthrough sheet if you're curious about when they get unlocked. Achievement-related steps are marked in orange.

Is there anything missable in Elden Ring?

Unfortunately a lot of things are missable. While mostly quest-related, two achievements can be permanently missed until your next playthrough. All missable entries are marked with MISSABLE, so you can easily use the search bar to only display these steps. Optional steps, or missable entries that don't give any reward, are marked with Optional. You won't miss anything if you're following the Walkthrough.

What level should I be for each area?

I provide recommended level ranges for each area based on my walkthrough runs where I intentionally stay underleveled, quitting out to avoid enemies in the open world and only engaging in combat inside of legacy dungeons. These ranges don't necessarily represent the difficulty of an area, but rather the level you're likely to reach naturally when following the walkthrough. They are suggestions based on my own characters, and could help you avoid being over- or under-leveled if you want a fairer experience.

I accidentally attacked an NPC, what should I do?

You should never attack or kill NPCs unless it's specifically stated to do so. If you attacked an NPC and they're now hostile, you can travel to the Church of Vows and absolve your sins at the statue inside (costs 1 Celestial Dew). If you've killed an NPC, then their questline is over until you start your next playthrough. Make sure to pick up their Bell Bearing if they were selling items. You may also want to check the questline of your affected NPC in the Questlines sheet.

How do I follow your directions?

Elden Ring uses cardinal, intercardinal and secondary-intercardinal directions. This means the compass can be broken down into a total of 16 directions:

  1. Cardinal Directions:
    • North
    • East
    • South
    • West
  2. Intercardinal Directions:
    • Northeast
    • Southeast
    • Southwest
    • Northwest
  3. Secondary Intercardinal Directions:
    • North-Northeast
    • East-Northeast
    • East-Southeast
    • South-Southeast
    • South-Southwest
    • West-Southwest
    • West-Northwest
    • North-Northwest

For an in-game example, northeast would refer to the middle point between north and east. Using the same logic, north-northeast would refer to the middle point between north and northeast, whereas east-northeast would be the middle point between east and northeast. [See visual example]

Can I use this for multiple characters?

Yep, you can create, edit, and delete profiles on the Options page. You can also use the NG+ button to delete progress for only the Walkthrough, Questlines, Bosses and New Game Plus sheets specifically. Your progress will be saved to the currently selected profile.

How is my progress saved?

Your progress is stored locally in your browser. This means that clearing your browser's cache will destroy any saved progress.

To back up your data, go to Options and select Backup as file. This will export your data as a JSON file. You can then import your progress on any device by selecting Restore from file and choosing the JSON file. On mobile devices, JSON files aren't supported, so use the clipboard options instead to sync your progress.
